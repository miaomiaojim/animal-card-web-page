Fennec Fox

Its unusual large ears serve to dissipate heat in desert environment and locate vertebrates.

  • Scientific Name: Vulpes zerda
  • Appearance
    • The fur of the fennec fox is straw-colored.
    • Its tapering tail has a black tip .
    • Its long ears have longitudinal reddish strips on the back and so densely haired inside that the external auditory meatus is not visible.
    • The edges of the ears are whitish, but darker on the back.
    • The ear to body ratio is the greatest in the canid family.
    • It has streaks runnning from the inner eye to either side of the slender muzzle.
    • The pad of its paws are covered with dense fur.
  • Length-the smallest canid
    • Females
      • head to body (34.5-39.5cm)
      • tail (23-25cm)
    • Males
      • head to body (39-39.5cm)
      • tail (23-25cm)
  • Distribution and Habitat
    • Distribution
      • Sahara
      • Egypt
      • the Sinai Peninsula
      • the Arava and Negev deserts
    • Habitat
      • small sand dunes
      • vast treeless sand areas with sparse vegetation such as grasses, sedges and shrubs
  • Longest Lifespan
    • Females (13 years)
    • Males (14 years)
  • Diet-omnivore
    • small rodents, lizards, geckos, skinks, smal birds and their eggs
    • insects
    • fruits and tubers
    • water
  • Predators
    • African horned owl species
    • (anecdotal reports) caracals, jackals and striped hyenas

The straw-colored fennec fox lived in the deserts of North Africa. Its coat, ears, and kidney functions have adapted to the desert environment wih high temperatures and little waterand it's the smallest canid. It has a life span of up to 14 years in captivity and about 10 years in the wild.
